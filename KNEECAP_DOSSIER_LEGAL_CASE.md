## SECTION II: Paramilitary Imagery & Glorification of Violence

### 2.1 Band Name Origin

"Kneecap" refers to "kneecapping" - a brutal paramilitary punishment practice in Northern Ireland involving shooting victims in the knees, historically used against alleged drug dealers by both loyalist and republican paramilitaries during the Troubles.

**Context:**
- [Wikipedia: Paramilitary punishment attacks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramilitary_punishment_attacks_in_Northern_Ireland)
- The practice has left lasting trauma on Belfast communities
- Use of this name trivialises serious violence

---

### 2.2 Balaclava Merchandise (2024)

**Products Sold:**
- Balaclavas in Irish tricolour colours
- Images of burning police Land Rovers
- Paramilitary-associated imagery marketed to young people

**Criticism:**
- Normalizes masks associated with paramilitary violence and criminal concealment
- Belfast community groups expressed concern about glorification of paramilitarism
- [BBC: "Kneecap are no strangers to controversy"](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly5r4gg7l6o)

**Police Response (Dec 2024):**
- Group claimed they were "banned" from selling merchandise at Belfast SSE Arena
- Police rubbished the "merch ban" claim
- [Belfast Telegraph: Police rubbish merch ban claim](https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/northern-ireland/kneecap-claim-they-are-banned-from-selling-controversial-merch-at-sse-arena-ahead-of-big-belfast-gig-this-weekend/a952416217.html)

## SECTION III: Drug Culture Glamorization

### 3.1 Biographical Film - Drug Dealing Portrayal

**Film:** *Kneecap* (2024), directed by Rich Peppiatt

**Content:**
- Film depicts band members as "low-level drug dealers" in Belfast
- Features "running gags about drugs" throughout
- Shows scenes of prescription medication abuse
- ABC News explicitly describes it as "a biopic about **drug-dealing rappers**"

**Sources:**
- [ABC News: "How a biopic about drug-dealing rappers became 2024's most important Irish-language movie"](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2024-09-13/kneecap-irish-language-rap-movie-interview-controversy/104342738)
- [Guardian review](https://www.theguardian.com/film/article/2024/aug/24/kneecap-review-one-of-the-funniest-films-of-the-year-rich-peppiatt-michael-fassbender-belfast)

**Band's Own Words (ABC Interview):**
> "We like to keep the lines blurred, but as we say, some of the crazier stories in the movie are true"

**Concerns:**
- glamorizes drug trade that destroys Belfast communities
- normalises prescription drug abuse
- presents drug dealing as rebellious/cool to young audiences
